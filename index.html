UI/UX Projects

Maynooth Furniture

Maynooth Furniture is one of the first projects that I embarked on while learning UI design. It was a fun and great experience; it offered a challenge, which I love. This project taught me valuable lessons on the concepts of prototyping and interactivity. Basically trying to mimic the feel of the final product. A persona was created for this project, and it was based on someone who loved interior decor but was mindful about their pocketsâ€”someone not too old with a little family. All these spoke to me in the process of creating this; the choice of colors and minimalist design style for this project were all a result of trying to satisfy such a character.

Kitchenware Commerce

This project was a collaboration with another web developer. Although it did not take up its final form as an e-commerce website, it was a great experience for me because of the time constraints that I was able to meet. The client required that the site have an "Amazon-esque" feel in terms of a multi-level navbar, characterized by the presence of a search bar and categories for the available products. They were also specific with the colors since they wanted something that was in line with their brand. I therefore decided to go for their needs but with a little twist. I tried to achieve a futuristic feel that would capture the hearts of the young and the young at heart. Something that would stand out from all the competitors in that market segment.

Color Admin Design System

"Color Admin" is an administrator dashboard that helps administrators monitor various metrics and statistics at a glance. The norm at my work place is to design a rough sketch and edit the "Color Admin" programatically; however, there was one customer who presented a great challenge with respect to the completion time requirements of his project. There needed to be frequent communication with this client to make sure he was satisfied; therefore, the solution was to replicate the color admin interface on Figma to get all the technicalities right before proceeding to code. I was tasked with this, and I started by creating the components that are present on the "Color Admin" template itself; this aided the team in creating the various pages easily.

Frontend Projects

Mansah Media

This project was another exciting one that presented a challenge. A first meeting with the client established the fact that she needed something that represented feminism in a bold yet elegant manner. I pondered over these pointers and decided on the colors and style to use. I went with a youthful purple look that satisfied the needs of the client. She was wowed when she saw how the site turned out and acknowledged the fact that the site exceeded her expectations. Church Management System

"The Church Management System" is one that was meant to bring reliability, efficiency, speed, and ease of use to the "house of God". It is a one-stop solution for all the basic needs of churches within Ghana and beyond. While the actual project was being worked on, I was tasked with creating the landing page to sell the product. What will help a product sell? In this context, the website would have to be convincing enough in summary. That was the goal when I was working on this website. I made sure to capture the attention of potential clients with the design and convince them that they need the system with the information present on the website. I was pleased with myself after completing this project, as it was undoubtedly beautiful.

Shea Butter Commerce

"Shea Butter Commerce" is another project that did not come to fruition because of some technical difficulties on the client's end. The client needed a classy commerce site to sell shea butter, which is very useful to humans. The mindset I had with this one was to capture the various colors that shea butter represents. I then made these colors communicate in such a way that the target user would be drawn to make purchases. I took away valuable lessons from this project, as the time constraints were scanty. I can proudly say that this project was completed in a very short time with highly desirable results.

CSS Framework

BaseCSS

"BaseCSS" is short for basic CSS. This is a boilerplate or framework that I made with the beginner in mind. The idea came about when I was learning JavaScript. I wanted to create an avenue for new developers to be able to learn JavaScript with style, meaning I wanted to empower developers to learn JavaScript without being too rigid in terms of design, where they can style their webpages with ease and without taking the focus off the concepts they may be trying to grasp. It focuses on the fundamentals of CSS but in an easy and efficient manner. It is light-weight, concise, and precise. Anyone with an interest in web development can pick it up.
